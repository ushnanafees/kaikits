About Us

POSITIVE BEHAVIOURS WRAPPED IN A CONVENIENT KIT

Did you know that for every second, minute, hour that your child is at play or away from you they can be exposed to many illnesses such as the common cold and flu or more severe Hand, Foot and Mouth Disease?

Did you also know that many of these illnesses could be reduced if children were encouraged to protect themselves with proper health practices at all times?

Kai Kit for Kids was born out of the need to encourage and empower children to be personally involved with protecting their own health.

Our kits are designed by kids, tested by kids and is suitable for kids! They are just the right size to fit into school backpacks, grandma weekend backpacks, long road trip backpacks and even hand luggage for those longer trips away from home.

We believe that by making health care personal for children, we will encourage positive behaviours that will last to adulthood.

Grab a Kai Kit for Kids today! Freshen up, sanitize and protect everywhere you go!

I'm Kailee, a 9 year old girl

who loves all things unicorn and emojis. I decided to start Kai Kit for Kids because I realized that sometimes when children are at school, we may have messy situations and may be too frightened or just do not know what to do.

When my mommy gave me a “just in case Kit” it was very helpful for times when I needed things such as hand soap or wipes. She even included a sanitary napkin and started teaching me about how to use this.

My friends were surprised that I had a sanitary napkin and asked me about this, so I told them that some little girls may start their periods at an early age and we all needed to be prepared. My friends were curious and excited and wanted to have their own kit and this is when I decided that I wanted to create a kit to help all children to be clean and healthy at all times.

So, Imagine that you are at school and you have a messy situation. You can always reach into your Kai Kit for one of the many things that will help you be be clean and healthy because you just never know when stuff will happen.
